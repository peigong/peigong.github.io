# The Temple of Heaven

## The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest

###  Overview

Ladies and gentlemen, friends, I will be your guide today.

And it's my great honor to show you around the Temple of Heaven.

We are now in front of the east gate of the Temple of Heaven, And I'd like to give you a brief introduction before our entry.

The Temple of Heaven was built in 1420 during the reign of Emperor of the Ming Dynasty.

It was not only rated as the largest group of temple building in China, but also the largest heaven worshipping architecture in the world.

The coverage of the whole area is over 273 hectares. The main buildings in the Temple of Heaven are circular shaped. But, each building has a round and a square wall around it. This layout shows the ancient people's belief that heaven was round while the earth was square.

Originally, the Temple of Heaven was called Temple of Heaven and Earth, because it was built according to the Temple of Heaven and Earth in Nanjing, the early Ming capital at that time, so both the heaven and the earth were worshipped here. In 1530, another structure, the Temple of Earth was built in the northern part of Beijing and then Heaven and Earth were worshipped separately.

Later Emperor Qianlong had it enlarged and made it the largest royal altar. The compound was damaged twice. one in 1860 and the other in 1900 by foreign invaders. In 1918, it was opened the public. In 1998, it was listed by UNESCO as one of the world heritages.

There is an interesting way to remember the characteristic of Temple of Heaven, which is one, three, five, seven and nine, let me explain the numbers for you.

'One' refers to the one central line running from north to south connecting 11 buildings.

'Three' refers to the three altar walls, the outer temple wall, the inner temple wall and the partition wall separating the Circular Mound Altar from the Altar of Prayer for Grain.

'Five' refers to the five major architectural complexes. They are the Circular Mound of Altar, the Altar of Prayer for Grain, The Palace of Abstinence, the Divine Music Administration and the Sacrificial Hall.

'Seven' refers to the Seven Star Stones on the east end of the Long Corridor.

'Nine' stands for nine temple gates: two on the outer wall, four around the the Circular Mound Altar and three around the Altar of Prayer for Grain.

### The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest

Now let me introduce you the main building of the Temple of Heaven, The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest, which is located on the north end along the central axis. It is one of beijing's symbolic structures and also the most famous temple structure in the world. In the old days, it was the place where the emperors in the Ming and Qing dynasty came on the 15th day of the first lunar month of every year to pray for a good harvest.

The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests was first built in 1420, and the current one was rebuilt in 1896. This unique building with its Chinese style wooden architecture, is not only splendid in outer appearance, but also unique in its inner frame. It is completely made of wood without any use of steel, cement or even the use of a single nail.

The entire structure is supported by 28 massive wooden pillars, and all pillars inside the hall have special significance. The 28 wooden pillars symbolize 28 constellations in the sky. They are arranged in three rings.

The four pillars in the center are the thickest and painted with gilded flowers to make the first ring, known as the Dragon-well Pillars. The space between each of the four pillars symbolizes the four seasons of the year.

Encircling the Dragon-well Pillars is the second ring, which consists of 12 pillars known as the Golden Pillars. Each space between the 12 pillars represents 12 months of the year.

The third ring has another 12 pillars named EavePpillars. Each space between these pillars Symbolizes the 12 divisions of day and night.

The 12 golden pillars plus the 12 Eave-Pillars symbolize 24 solar terms in traditional Chinese lunar calendar.

The floor is paved with nine layers of green marble flagstones. In the center of the floor, there is a big round marble stone called the Dragon and Phoenix stone, on which the emperor would stand and kneel down to pray for a good harvest during the worshipping ceremony. To the north of the Dragon and Phoenix stone placed the tablet of the God of Heaven on a white marble pedestal while the tablets of the Emperor's ancestors on both sides.

On the top, at the center of the caisson ceiling, there are inlaid relief sculptures of a wriggling dragon and a dancing phoenix corresponding to the Dragon and Phoenix Stone on the floor.

To the east of the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests runs a long corridor, which links the Divine Kitchen and Divine Warehouse together. What were they used for?

The Divine Kitchen was the place for preparing offerings for the worshipped the gods, while the Divine Warehouse was for storing the articles and offerings for the ceremony. The long corridor was for transporting the sacrificial offerings from the kitchen to the warehouse to keep them clean from the wind and dust. It is 350 meters long and 5 meters wide with 72 sections.

### The Seven Star stones

To the southeast of the long corridor lie the Seven Star Stones symbolizing the seven peaks of the Mount Tai. Later, Qing Emperor Qianlong had the 8th stone added at the northeast corner in the Hope of a harmonious relationship between the Han group and the Manchu Ethnic group.

### The Red Stairway Bridge

Finally, let's have a look at the Red Stairway Bridge, which links the complex of Circular Mound Altar in the south and the complex of the Altar for Praying for Grain in the north up together.

It is 360 meters long and 29.4 meters wide, and its north end is 2 meters higher than the south end with the  wish of ascending to heaven for good luck.

There are three highlighted paths along the road. The middle one was reserved for the God of Heaven, the ruler in the Heaven. The western path was reserved for male officials, while the eastern one was for the emperor only.

## The Circular Mound Altar

### Overview

Ladies and gentlemen, friends, I will be your guide today. And it's my great honor to show you around the Circular Mound Altar of the Temple of Heaven.

We are now in front of the south gate of the Temple of Heaven. And I'd like to give you a brief introduction before our entry.

The three-layered white marble altar in front of us is called Circular Mound Altar. Built in 1530, it was 5.71 meters high and used to be a place for the emperors to worship the God of Heaven. It is enclosed by double walls. The inner wall is round shaped while the outer wall is square. That reflects the belief that people believed the shape of heaven was round, while the shape of earth was square.

In ancient times, on winter solstice, emperor would come here and offer some solemn sacrifices on it to worship heaven. During the ceremony, the emperor would report to the god of the heaven about the previous year's good harvest, and the country prosperity, and prayed for continuous prosperity for the coming year. Sometimes the emperor had to come again and pray for rain if there was drought in that year.

The architecture of its style can be described by numbers. It is located on a 3-layered round marble base. The Top layer is nine Zhang in diameter(Zhang is an ancient Chinese calculating measurement). The second layer is 15 zhang in diameter, which is three times five, and the 3rd layer is 21 Zhang, which is three times seven. All these showed 1, 3, 5, 7, and nine altogether. And the numbers of the three diameter Add up to 45 in total.

In the ancient times, The Chinese believed that 1, 3, 5, 7 and nine were 'Yang numbers' that had special powers. Of all of them, Nine ranks the highest meaning supreme power. According to the Book of Changes, the number 45 symbolizes supreme power and the dignity of the emperor. Do you know why? Because 45 comes from Nine times five and it connects to the Chinese version 'Jiu Wu Zhi Zun'(the symbol of emperor's power).

### Heavenly Heart Stone

There is a piece of round stone known as Heavenly Heart Stone. It is surrounded by nine circles of fan-shaped stone slabs. The first circle consists of nine stone slabs, 18 for the second circle, 27 for the 3rd circle ...... and 81 for the 9th circle. Even the numbers of baluster slabs on each layer are also multiples of nine. There are a total of 360 balusters, which stand for the 360 degrees of circumference of heaven.

Nowadays, when we look at these balusters, I think it is not only about the meaning of number nine, But also a magnificent piece of art. There are nine steps on each terrace leading down in four directions. The whole design of the Altar makes us feel much closer to heaven.

At the southeast of the Circular Mound Altar, there is a round shape platform made of green glazed bricks with nine stairs in the south, east and west. It is called Firewood Stove. After the worshipping ceremony, emperor would stand on a platform to watch the burning of all the offerings to the God of Heaven.

## Imperial Vault of Heaven

### Imperial Vault of Heaven

Now, let's have a look at the Imperial Vault of Heaven. It was first built in 1530 and remained unchanged since the restoration in 1752.

It's located on a marble base, which is 19.2 meters in height and 15.6 meters in diameter. The roof is covered by blue glazed tiles. There are eight pillars in both inner and outer circles that support the roof. The door that opens to the south employees a wooden structure, but the structures to the east, west and north are made of brick. If seen from a distance, all these characteristics make the building look like a sapphire umbrella with a golden crown.

The Imperial Vault of Heaven was the place to keep the tablet of the God of the Heaven and the tablets of the emperor's ancestors. Inside the building, There is a tablet with a gold-gilded inscription of the God of Heaven in both Chinese languages of Han and Manchu together with four tablets of the Qing emperor's ancestors placed on each side. Emperor would come and invite his ancestors to join the worshipping ceremony one day before in order to show his respects and after the ceremony the tablets would be sent back.

### The Echo Wall

The Echo Wall is a circular wall surrounding the Imperial Vault of Heaven. The wall is 3.7 meters high, 90 centimeters thick and 193.2 meters long. If one person speaks in his normal voice, or even whispers at any point close to the wall, the wall will send the sound traveling along the wall and another person will be able to hear the voice clearly at the other end.

The reason for the wall to make this possible is: the wall is round, It has eaves on the top, it is hard and smooth on the surface, and all the bricks are hermetically laid. Because of the conductivity of sound waves, it's easy to send the sound traveling along the wall.

### Triple-sound Stone

In front of the Imperial Vault of Heaven, there are three stones known as Triple-sound Stone. If you stand on the first stone and clap your hands, the echo can be heard once; a handclap on the second stone can produce the echoes twice, and if you do the same thing on the 3rd stone, the echoes can be heard three times.

This is because the three flagstones are at different distances from the Echo Wall and the sound waves are reflected by the wall & bounced back to the center and different times. This sound-echoing phenomenon was enveloped in mystery in the old days. It was believed that 'a whisper in the Earth sounds like a thunder in Heaven', which means that every word and behavior in the human world could be clearly perceived by Heaven's sharp eyes.

## The Heaven-worshipping Ceremony

Now let me introduce the Heaven-worshipping Ceremony to you.

Every year on the winter solstice day, emperor would come here to hold a worshipping ceremony. He would come to the Hall of Abstinence and stay three days before the ceremony. Ever since the reign of emperor Qianlong of the Qing Dynasty the routines had changed. As a result of the changes, the emperor stayed at the Hall of Abstinence for two days first in the Forbidden City and then one day at the Temple of Heaven.

One day before the ceremony, emperor would go to the Imperial Vault of Heaven show his respect to his ancestors by burning the incense in front of the tablets. Then he would also go to the Divine Kitchen and the Divine Storehouse of the gods to check if sacrificial offerings were well prepared. After that, he would go to the Circular Mound Altar to show his respect and then return to the Hall of Abstinence.

On the ceremony day, the bell at the Supreme Harmony Bell would be rung while the emperor traveled from the Hall of Abstinence to the Altar. However, he would go to the Dressing Platform first to change his uniform and then he went to the Circular Mound Altar while waiting for the offerings got ready.

The Ceremony began in the early morning. Emperor would lead military and civil officials to the altar and then start to offer the sacrifices on it. While the usher was chanting, the fire in the ovens would be lit to burn sacrificial calf. When incense and cypress trees were burned, the surrounding areas were bathed in smoke, and it created a solemn and mysterious atmosphere.

During the ceremony, emperor would stand on the second step of the Altar. The performers would beat the drums and play various music instruments. When emperor presented offerings, singing and dancing were conducted.

After the ceremony, emperor would go back to the Forbidden City to receive greetings and the divine name tablets would be returned to the Imperial Vault of Heaven.
